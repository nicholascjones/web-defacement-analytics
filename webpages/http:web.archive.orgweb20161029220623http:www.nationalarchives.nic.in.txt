The National Archives of India is the repository of the non-current records of
the Government of India and is holding them in trust for the use of
administrators and scholars. It is an Attached Office of theMinistry of
Culture. It was set up in March 1891 in Calcutta (Kolkata) as the Imperial
Record Department and subsequent to the transfer of the National Capital from
Calcutta to New Delhi in 1911 it was shifted to its present building in 1926.
